Exploring computational modelling & generative AI for the social sciences

The Synthetic Social Science Group is a new interdisciplinary working group at Oxford that explores the frontiers of computational modelling and generative AI for the social sciences. We meet regularly during term-time and welcome researchers from across the university and beyond who are interested in large language models (LLMs) for social science, agent-based models (ABMs), generative social science, human-computer interaction, and the construction of artificial societies.

What is the group?

The group brings together perspectives from political science, sociology, computer science, philosophy, and beyond, with the goal of building a space for exploring the theoretical and empirical challenges posed by synthetic agents and computational simulations of society.

Who is it for?

Attendance is open to graduate students, researchers, and academic staff. If you are interested in joining, please fill out the form below or contact us to be added to the mailing list and receive the term card.

Reading Group

Reading group sessions will feature guided discussions of new texts and methods in this emerging field or a practical session to try out implementing the previously discussed approach.

  • New methods & seminal papers
  • Hands-on practicals
  • Collaborative notes

Working Group

Working group sessions will feature a mix of invited speakers and work-in-progress presentations from group members.

  • Invited talks
  • Work-in-progress presentations
  • Feedback & collaboration
